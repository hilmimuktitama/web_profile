## Context

This note keeps the private setup out and focuses on the operating pattern.

The useful question was not "can AI write the update?" It was "can the team get to a reviewable first draft faster without blurring what is known, unknown, or still waiting on a person?"

## What changed

- Planning notes and task context were turned into draft updates more quickly.
- Drafts kept source references and explicit unknowns visible.
- Nothing published automatically.
- The TPM still owned the final judgment; AI only reduced the blank-page work.

## Evidence I can show

- Sanitized prompt structure for turning source notes into reviewable planning drafts.
- A source-check pattern that labels facts, assumptions, missing inputs, and human decisions separately.
- Example review criteria for deciding whether an AI-assisted draft is usable, incomplete, or misleading.
