## Context

This case note keeps the private details out and focuses on the shape of the work: planning context, blocker visibility, owners, dates, and stakeholder updates were spread across too many places.

The operating risk was not only missed delivery. It was decision drift: people could attend the same review and leave with different assumptions about what was blocked, who owned it, and what changed since the last update.

## What changed

- Pulled scattered delivery signals into a simpler weekly review rhythm.
- Separated facts, assumptions, open decisions, blockers, owners, dates, and next actions.
- Reduced status narration by making the written update clear enough to review before the meeting.
- Made follow-through visible by carrying unresolved items forward until they had a next move.

## Evidence I can show

- Sanitized operating-model outline for review cadence, owner tracking, and escalation.
- Before/after examples of status updates with sensitive names and metrics removed.
- A checklist for keeping planning artifacts close to source evidence instead of summary memory.
